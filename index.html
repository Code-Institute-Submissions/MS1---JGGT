About Me

My obsession with Music began from a young age, I started playing guitar at age 8 and went on to study Music at the highest level at The Institute of Contempory Music Performance in London. After my time there I went on to perform across the UK and abroad on various gigs, tours and festivals. With all this experience I'm able to teach any level of guitar, whether you might be a complete beginner, a seasoned shred head or somewhere inbetween!

If you're interested in taking your guitar playing to the next level and for more information about availability and pricing then head to the Contact page!

Establish Goals

What do you want to achieve? Set your sights on what you want to get out of your tuition. It might be something as simple as learning a specific song or solo, it might be taking your RockSchool exams or applying for a music college.

Track Progress

I have always found that by tracking students progress, they have a clear way of seeing and measuring where they started and how far they've come. This is really important especially when students might start to lose focus in why they're studying. By seeing their past progress it can help bolster them when they hit a steep learning curve and realise that they can push through.

Results!

Last but not least, it's crucially important to quantify results to give students a sense of accomplishment. The feeling when you get finally "get" it, when all the little pieces of that puzzle finally click into place is hugely rewarding.

Media

This first track is from my band When Our Time Comes' debut self titled album. It's more of a rhythmically challenging song, particularly the bridge section. The bridge starts off with a clean guitar part arpeggiating the chorus chords before kicking into a heavy alternate picked 16th note riff that bounces back and forth between 4/4 and 3/4 time every bar. At a tempo of 148bpm it can be a very fatiguing passage for the right hand to play and required a lot of metronome practice to get it sounding tight!
This track is another When Our Time Comes song, this time from our debut EP. This again comes with some more rhythmically challenging aspects with most of the song being in 7/8 time as well as some big chord shapes in the chorus. The solo features some advanced techniques such as two handed tapping and some fast legato runs over more odd time signatures.
